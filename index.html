Mecklenburg County Quality of Life

November 2022

Despite the economic setbacks of a global pandemic, Mecklenburg County has continued to set the pace for economic growth in the US.

Character

Population Density

Although population growth has slowed, population density has seen a steady increase since 2000 and now averages 3 people per acre.

Character

Vacant Land

Vacant land in Mecklenburg County has dropped to 16.1%, with a much of that land pushed toward the periphery.

Character

Race/Ethnicity

Race/Ethnicity - Hispanic or Latino has seen a steady increase, and stands at 15.2% in 2020.

Character

Race/Ethnicity

Race/Ethnicity - White or Caucasian continues a decline in population percentage, dropping to 44.7% in 2020.

Economy

Job Density

Job Density continues to increase in Mecklenburg County, oriented primarily along major transportation corridors. 95.2% of adults in the labor force were employed in 2020.

Economy

Commercial Space

While Commercial Space increased to 464M SqFt in 2020, the average square footage space per parcel has dropped considerably since 2017 as commercial space becomes more disaggregated in the County.

Economy

High Speed Internet Access

In 2020, 79% of Mecklenburg County residents have High Speed Internet Access, with a corridor below I-85 and above downtown Charlotte with the least access despite high population density. Roughly 110,000 people in Mecklenburg County have no internet access at all.

Education

Education Level - Bachelor's Degree

45.8% of adults over the age of 25 have a bachelor's degree or higher. Mecklenburg County is significantly higher than North Carolina as a whole (28%), but ranked 28th among large metro areas in a 2012 study. The county shows a distinct band of lower performance, which occurs on a number of education metrics.

Education

Test Proficiency - Elementary School

Proficency in End of Grade testing in elementary school has remained steady since 2014, following closely with the national average.

Education

Student Absenteeism

13.5% of CMS students were absent 10 or more days during 2019, and increase from 8.2% in 2013. Absenteeism varies greatly over the county, with some neighborhood profile areas exceeding 30%.

Engagement

311 Requests

Mecklenburg County has seen an increase in 311 requests, averaging 32 calls per 100 people. 311 handled 355,490 requests in 2021, a 46.4% increase in just five years.
